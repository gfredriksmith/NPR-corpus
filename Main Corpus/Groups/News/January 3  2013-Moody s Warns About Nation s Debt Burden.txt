 NPR's business news starts with a warning from Moody's.   Congress may have approved a bill that averted the crisis - the manufactured crisis known as the fiscal cliff - but the credit-rating agency Moody's is not patting lawmakers on the back. Moody's said this weeks' deficit reduction deal did not produce meaningful improvement to the issue at hand the country's debt burden and economic output. Moody's warned that if improvements are not made the agency could downgrade the U.S. credit rating.
