 NPR's business news starts with holiday shopping.   We are not really in the holiday shopping season yet but retailers are already looking forward to it and hoping it will be better than recent seasons. A new forecast from the National Retail Federation this morning predicts a modest 2.3 percent bump in holiday sales. If that prediction comes true it would be the biggest increase in sales in four years. But it is not as if consumers are likely to go on a spending spree. Another survey from the consulting firm Accenture reports that 83 percent of consumers expect to spend the same or less on gifts this year than last year.
