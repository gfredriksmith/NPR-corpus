 NPR's business news starts with the latest buzz on Twitter.   Britain's Financial Times reports that JP Morgan Chase a company that bears the name of a guy who famously put together steel companies shipping lines and railroads - JP Morgan Chase is considering a 10 percent stake in the social media company Twitter. Talks are currently under way and the banking company is looking at a possible investment of around 450 million according to the report. If the deal goes through it would be the most prominent investment yet by JP Morgan's new digital growth fund. Twitter like a lot of emerging social media operations is still a private company.
