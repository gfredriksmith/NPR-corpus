 Good morning. I am Renee Montagne. In Japan a sewage treatment plant has turned out to be a gold mine literally. Officials say they have retrieved nearly two kilograms of gold per metric ton of ash from the sludge they incinerate. That is almost 50 times what miners find in Japan's Hishikari mine one of the top gold mines in the world. Plant officials believe they are getting so much gold because manufacturers in the area use the precious metal in making precision instruments. It is MORNING EDITION.
