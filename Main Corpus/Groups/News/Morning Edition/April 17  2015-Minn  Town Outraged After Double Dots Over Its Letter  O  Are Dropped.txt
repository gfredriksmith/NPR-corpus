 Good morning I am David Greene. Minnesota's Department of Transportation was just following federal spelling guidelines when it replaced the road signs outside the tiny town of Lindstrom. They removed the pronunciation mark known as the umlaut. That is the pair of dots above the O in the city's name. The residents of this area with deep Scandinavian roots were so angry they were seeing spots. The governor was mad too. When the news broke he issued an executive order for the DOT to put the dots back. You are listening to MORNING EDITION.
